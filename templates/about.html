Story

I was around eleven years old when my parents purchased our first ISP subscription and I instantly became hooked on the internet. I spent most of my time making basic geocities sites, skinning Winamp, and pranking my friends with Trojan horse programs such as Sub7. The summer before high school a close friend introduced me to linux. I started on Mandrake, and from there I’ve never looked back. Through the help of IRC and support forums I spent my 9th and 10th grade years learning “how to Linux”, such things like compiling a custom linux kernel, and doing a stage one Gentoo install. Linux soon became my daily driver.

Tech was always just a hobby until around 2011 when a friend suggested I do it professionally. I started part time contracting Desktop Support, and from there I worked in a few other support roles until I got a break in 2015.

I was offered a Jr. SysAdmin role at a small company. It was there that I really grew up professionally. Since the company was young I had to wear many hats; internal IT, data center ops, linux administration, and code deployments to name a few. As the company matured, my role became more defined and towards the end of my tenure I was primarily focused on Linux administration and code deployments.

Wanting to learn more about the cloud computing, I accepted a job as a DevOps Engineer at my current company working with tech such as Kubertetes, AWS, and Terraform.
